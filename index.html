Roadmap for Scaling Impact Investment in Urban Food Systems

Impact

Impact is an overall performance standard. An investment intended to generate impact aims to achieve specific net positive social or environmental externalities, or secondary effects.

Ambition

Ambition in relation to investment for impact can mean clear targets and timelines, such as conserving 30% of land-based and marine ecosystems by 2030 or achieving net zero emissions by mid-century.

Learn More

Data

Data systems need to evolve, connect, and integrate new kinds of information to better reflect new intended areas of food systems impact, revealing investment needs.

Learn More

Policy

Policy actions shape the environment in which investment for impact will operate, recognizing the forces that block innovators and shifting incentives to welcome better practices.

Learn More

Products

Products, tools, and services facilitate the flow of new investment for impact, providing a key step in facilitating investment and process readiness.

Learn More

Scaling

Scaling strategies allow local and community-level actors to reach a wider market, giving investors a clear pathway to higher financial return on specific impact investment interventions.

Learn More

Capacity

Building capacity involves situating the appropriate expertise at the right point along the value chain, including the mentality and food culture of consumers, communities, and city-level policymakers.

Learn More

Community

Food systems live in our homes and communities. An optimal investment trajectory must operate through effective evolution of everyday practice in communities, ensuring cultural sensitivity and sustainability.
